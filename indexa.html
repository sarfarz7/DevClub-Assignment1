FAQ's

What is covid 19?

oronaviruses are a type of virus. There are many different kinds, and some cause disease. A coronavirus identified in 2019, SARS-CoV-2, has caused a pandemic of respiratory illness, called COVID-19.

Should I use soap and water or hand sanitizer to protect myself?

CDC recommends washing hands with soap and water whenever possible because handwashing reduces the amounts of all types of germs and chemicals on hands. But if soap and water are not available, using a hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol can help you avoid getting sick and spreading germs to others.

What are the symptoms and complications that Covid-19 can cause?

Frequent Symptoms Although people with COVID-19 may have many symptoms, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) lists 11 of the most common. Symptoms typically appear between 2 and 14 days after infection:1 Fever or chills Cough Shortness of breath Fatigue Muscle and body aches Headache Loss of taste or smell Sore throat Congestion or runny nose Nausea or vomiting Diarrhea


Complications from severe COVID-19 include the following:11 Pneumonia Acute respiratory distress syndrome Sepsis and septic shock Heart injury Acute kidney injury Although anyone can develop COVID-19, age increases your risk for severe complications.12 Individuals with certain underlying medical conditions are also at an increased risk for severe illness if they become infected with SARS-CoV-2
